Value Investing

"Price is what you pay. Value is what you get."

What is Value Investing?

Value investing is an investment paradigm that involves buying securities that appear underpriced by some form of fundamental analysis. Unlike speculators who focus on short-term price movements, value investors view stocks as partial ownership of a real business.

Core Principles

Intrinsic Value

The actual worth of a company based on its earnings, assets, and growth potential, independent of its current market price.

Margin of Safety

Buying a stock at a significant discount to its intrinsic value to minimize risk and allow for errors in estimation.

Mr. Market

An allegory created by Benjamin Graham: the market is a partner who offers to buy or sell shares daily, often driven by emotion rather than logic.

Long-term Horizon

Value investing requires patience. It can take years for the market price to converge with the company's true value.

The Legends

  • Benjamin Graham: Known as the "Father of Value Investing" and author of The Intelligent Investor.
  • Warren Buffett: The most famous practitioner, who integrated Graham's principles with a focus on "moats" (competitive advantages).
  • Charlie Munger: Advocated for the "Latticework of Mental Models" and high-quality business selection.
